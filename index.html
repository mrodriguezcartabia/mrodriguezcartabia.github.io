About Me

Hello! I'm Mauro Rodriguez Cartabia, a dedicated Mathematician and Researcher based in Buenos Aires, Argentina. My academic journey includes a PhD in Mathematical Sciences from FCEN, UBA, where my doctoral thesis focused on 'Models for Evolutionary Games.' I also hold a degree as an Actuary in Economics from UBA.

Currently, I serve as an Assistant Researcher at Conicet (IMAS) and as an Interim Adjunct Professor at the Department of Mathematics, FCEN, UBA. My research interests span dynamic systems with delay, evolutionary game theory, and mathematical modeling in biology and social dynamics. I am passionate about exploring complex mathematical problems and contributing to scientific understanding.

Beyond my research, I have extensive teaching experience at various levels, from university courses to high school. I believe in fostering a deep understanding of mathematics and its applications. I am also proficient in English, having completed advanced studies at the Cambridge Institute of English Culture.
